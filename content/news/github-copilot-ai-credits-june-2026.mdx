## What's Actually Changing

GitHub announced that on **June 1, 2026**, Copilot's pricing shifts from a single monthly fee to **AI Credits** — the same model Cursor moved to in 2025. Each credit covers a unit of model use; different models (Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1) burn credits at different rates.

The flat $10/mo "Copilot Pro" tier becomes a **bundle** that includes a fixed credit allotment. Additional credits can be topped up via metered billing.

## Why GitHub Is Doing This

Two reasons, mostly driven by the 2026 agentic AI shift:

1. **Agent mode is expensive.** Heavy users of Copilot's agent mode were burning Microsoft's gross margin. Flat fees don't survive contact with users running 20-minute autonomous loops.
2. **Model diversity is now table stakes.** Copilot now offers Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5, and Gemini 3.1 — each with very different per-token economics. A flat fee can't price that.

Cursor did this in 2025. Windsurf, Cline (pay-as-you-go via API), and the entire CLI-tool category have been usage-based from day one. Copilot is the last major holdout to convert.

## What To Do Before June 1, 2026

### Step 1 — Audit Your Current Usage

Open Copilot's usage dashboard and check the last 30 days. Note:
- Average daily completions
- Number of agent/chat sessions
- Largest single agent task duration

### Step 2 — Pick a Strategy

Three reasonable paths:

**A. Stay on Copilot, optimize.** Cap your credit allotment, default to cheaper models (Gemini Flash for autocomplete, Opus 4.7 only for agent tasks).

Yes — 2,000 free completions per month for Copilot's free tier remains unchanged as of the May 2026 announcement.

**Which models does Copilot offer in 2026?**
Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Ultra, and a handful of smaller models for autocomplete. Each burns credits at different rates.

**Should enterprises renegotiate Copilot contracts now?**
If your seats lean heavy on agent mode — yes. Ask for fixed-credit enterprise bundles before the June 1 switch.
