## The TL;DR

**Buy Midjourney if:** you do design, illustration, or art-direction work and want photorealistic, gallery-quality outputs with deep artistic control.
**Skip it if:** you need text inside images or strict prompt adherence — DALL-E 3 still wins there.

## How We Tested

I used Midjourney v6 for **30 days of real client work**: 12 brand mood boards, 5 product mockups, and 40+ social-ready visuals. I tracked usable-image-per-prompt rate and total cost.

---

## What Midjourney Got Right

1. **Photorealism is unmatched.** Skin, fabric, lighting — indistinguishable from a 50mm portrait shot.
2. **Style consistency.** `--sref` style references make brand-consistent runs trivial.
3. **Speed.** Average generation time: 35 seconds for a 4-image grid.

## What Midjourney Got Wrong

1. **Text rendering is still rough.** Don't expect to put a logo or readable typography in your image.
2. **Prompt adherence is loose.** It interprets liberally — sometimes a feature, sometimes a bug.
3. **No native API on the cheap plan.** You're using the Discord/Web UI.

---

## The ROI Math

- **$10/mo Basic plan** → ~200 generations/mo.
- For commercial designers, that's roughly $0.05 per image — well below stock photo licensing.

---

## Verdict: 4.8 / 5

If you're a designer or art director, Midjourney's the most cost-effective creative tool on the market. The only ding is text rendering — keep DALL-E 3 in your back pocket for marketing assets that need readable copy.
