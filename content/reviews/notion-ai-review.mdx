## The TL;DR

**Buy Notion AI if:** you already live in Notion and want zero-setup AI on top of your team or personal workspace.
**Skip it if:** you want data ownership or model choice — Obsidian Copilot is the alternative.

## How We Tested

I migrated my **200-note personal knowledge base** into Notion and used Notion AI daily for 30 days: summarization, "ask my notes" Q&A, translation, and inline writing.

---

## What Notion AI Got Right

1. **"Ask my workspace" is genuinely useful.** Cited answers from my actual notes.
2. **Inline writing is frictionless.** Highlight → Improve → Done.
3. **Translation quality is excellent.** Tested EN ↔ FR ↔ JP.

## What Notion AI Got Wrong

1. **Per-seat pricing stings.** $10/user/mo on top of your existing Notion plan adds up fast for teams.
2. **No model choice.** You can't swap to Claude or run things locally.
3. **Long doc summaries get shallow.** Past ~10k words, summaries lose key details.

---

## Verdict: 4.0 / 5

If Notion is already your second brain, the AI add-on is a no-brainer. If you're shopping for a knowledge tool, weigh it against Obsidian Copilot for data ownership.

## FAQ

**Is Notion AI included in the free plan?** No, it's a $10/mo per-user add-on.

**Can I trial Notion AI?** Yes — first 20 AI responses are free per workspace.

**Does it train on my data?** Notion's policy says no, your data is not used to train models.
